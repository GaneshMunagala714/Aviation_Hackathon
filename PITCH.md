> "Every team here today is going to show you how to save fuel.
> We're going to show you why that's only solving HALF the problem."

*[Pause for effect]*

> "Those white lines you see behind airplanes? Contrails.
> They cause 35% of aviation's total warming impact.
> Nearly as much as all the CO2 from burning jet fuel.
> And every other solution ignores them completely."

## THE PROBLEM (30 seconds)

> "Aviation produces 2.5% of global CO2 emissions. That's the number
> everyone knows. But aviation's TOTAL climate impact is almost double
> that -- because of contrails."

> "Contrails form when hot exhaust hits cold, humid air at cruise
> altitude. When they persist, they trap heat like thin clouds.
> The science is clear: contrails are responsible for 35% of
> aviation's warming. That's from a Nature study, not us making
> it up."

> "Current flight planning optimizes for fuel. Nobody optimizes
> for contrails. Until now."

## JUDGE Q&A PREP

### "How accurate is the contrail prediction?"
> "We use the Schmidt-Appleman criterion, the industry standard for
> contrail formation physics. Google used a similar approach validated
> on 2,400 real flights. In production, we'd integrate NOAA radiosonde
> data for real-time humidity at altitude."

### "How do you handle air traffic control constraints?"
> "Great question. Our current model suggests altitude changes within
> standard flight level increments (1,000 ft in RVSM airspace). The
> Google/AA study showed 9% of flights accepted the altitude change,
> proving it's operationally feasible. Full ATC integration would be
> the next step."

### "What's the fuel penalty for avoiding contrails?"
> "Google's research showed 0.11% average fuel increase for 73%
> warming reduction. That's about $5 more fuel per flight for
> thousands of kilograms of warming avoided. The ROI is enormous."

### "Why haven't airlines already done this?"
> "They're starting to. Google and American Airlines ran the first
> commercial trial in 2025. The technology is cutting-edge. We're
> making it accessible as a planning tool."

### "What data would you need in production?"
> "Three things: 1) NOAA upper-air humidity data (freely available),
> 2) Aircraft performance models (BADA from EUROCONTROL), 3) Real
> flight data for ML training. The weather data alone gives us
> useful contrail predictions."

### "How is this different from what Google built?"
> "Google focused on contrail avoidance only. We combine it with
> fuel optimization in a single system -- optimizing total climate
> impact. Our 4D trajectory optimizer considers fuel, contrails,
> wind, and altitude simultaneously."
